## 2. Market Overview & Trends

### 2.1 The Rise of "Idle"
- **Mobile:** Idle games have evolved from simple clickers to complex "Idle RPGs" (*AFK Journey*, *Legend of Mushroom*).
- **PC/Steam:** A distinct "Premium Idle" niche exists (*Melvor Idle*, *Loop Hero*, *Rusty's Retirement*). These players appreciate "respectful" monetization (one-time purchases) over aggressive gacha.
- **Demographics:**
  - **Age:** ~32 years old average (younger than MMORPGs).
  - **Session:** Short & frequent (~5 sessions/day, ~10 mins each) on mobile; Background/Second-monitor play on PC.

## 4. Monetization Strategy: "The Fair & The Whale"

### 4.1 The â‚¬5 "Founder's Charter" (Remove Annoyance)
Designed to convert the "D7 Engaged" player. It should feel like "unlocking the full game".
* **Price:** â‚¬4.99 - â‚¬5.99
* **Contents:**
  * **Remove Ads:** (If ads exist).
  * **Automation:** Auto-assign workers to last job on new day.
  * **Speed:** 2x Game Speed toggle.
  * **Efficiency:** Queue multiple actions (if activity queue is a mechanic).
  * **Cosmetic:** "Founder" badge in leaderboards.

### 4.2 The Whale Economy (Uncapped Spending)
Whales need meaningful things to buy that don't trivialize the PvE challenge for others.
* **Gacha/Recruitment:** Rare Heroes (Essential if using the "Founder + Mediocre Village" pillar). Whales chase the 1% founder stats.
* **Time Warps:** "Skip 4 Hours" / "Instant Finish Job". High value because V1 has *no offline progress*.
* **Resurrection/Safety:** "Phoenix Feather" to save a high-level founder from permadeath. High emotional value.
* **Seasons/Battle Pass:** Monthly track with resources + exclusive skins.

### 4.3 Ad Strategy (Optional but Recommended)
* **Reward Video ONLY:** Never forced interstitials.
* **Uses:** "Watch to reroll Tavern recruits", "Watch to speed up current job by 30m".

## 6. Actionable Recommendations

### Positioning & Design
1.  **"Darkest Dungeon meets Cookie Clicker":** Lean into the "Manage, Suffer, Rebuild" loop in marketing. It distinguishes you from generic idle games.
2.  **The "Active Idle" Problem:** Since V1 has *no offline progress*, you **must** make the active window satisfying. Consider a "Battery Saver" mode (black screen, low FPS) so players can leave it running on phones/desktops without guilt.
3.  **Telegraph Death:** Permadeath drives monetization (Resurrection items), but it *must* be fair. Give players "Escape Chance" indicators.

### Monetization
4.  **The "Welcome Bundle" Trap:** Don't show the â‚¬5 pack on Day 1. Let them play. Show it on **Day 3** or after their first "close call".
5.  **Seasonal Servers:** If you want whales to stay, reset the leaderboards every 3-4 months (keep cosmetics/account progress). New servers = New whale races.

#### Steam Pricing (Desktop-first)

For the **Steam PC version** (desktop-first, no offline progress, idle/management niche), the current working assumption is:

- **Initial target price:** **â‚¬9.99**
  - Matches a scope where Idle Incremental RPG offers:
    - A strong core loop (village management + idle autobattler) with ~10â€“30 hours of meaningful play for most players.
    - A clear but not massive meta-progression layer (1â€“2 main upgrade trees), without promising dozens of biomes/modes.
    - A stylised but not AAA art direction, with good readability and coherent fantasy.
  - Fits the implicit player expectation of a **focused indie game with a strong idea**, some rough edges allowed.

- **When would ~â‚¬14.99 be justified?**
  - Significantly larger perceived scope:
    - 30â€“50+ hours of content for a typical player.
    - Multiple distinct zones/acts or modes that materially change gameplay.
    - Deeper meta (more upgrade trees, NG+/challenge modes) and/or narrative layers.
  - Higher production values (art, audio, UX) and a clearer commitment to **post-launch updates**.

Working decision for now: **design and position Idle Incremental RPG V1 around the â‚¬9.99 band on Steam**, with the option to revisit pricing (e.g. 11.99â€“14.99) only if scope and polish grow beyond the current Phase 12 plan.
